## PHASE 4 — SALARY NEGOTIATION (Germany-Specific)

### Your Fair Market Range

| Level | Gross Annual | Monthly Gross | Net Monthly* |
|-------|-------------|---------------|-------------|
| Minimum Accept | €75,000 | €6,250 | ~€3,700 |
| **Fair Market (Your Level)** | **€90,000–100,000** | **€7,500–8,333** | **~€4,400–4,900** |
| Strong Ask | €105,000–115,000 | €8,750–9,583 | ~€5,100–5,500 |
| Blue Card Minimum (IT) | €45,300 | — | — |

*Net after ~42% tax + social security (married/single affects this)

### Negotiation Scripts

**When recruiter asks salary expectation:**
> "Based on my 17 years of experience in Java architecture, Spring Boot microservices,
> and cloud platforms, and given the current market for senior technical leads in
> [Munich/Frankfurt/Berlin], I am targeting €92,000–100,000 gross annually.
> I am open to discussing the full package including relocation support,
> visa processing, and any performance bonus structure."

**If they say budget is lower (e.g., €70,000):**
> "I appreciate you sharing that. My concern is that €70,000 is below the market
> rate for someone at my seniority and skill depth. Is there flexibility in the
> budget, or scope to revisit at the 6-month mark? I want to find a number
> that works for both sides."

**If offer comes in at €80,000:**
> "Thank you for the offer — I am genuinely interested in this role.
> The number I had in mind was closer to €95,000 based on my research
> and the scope of the role. Is there room to move to €90,000?
> I'd like to close this positively."

**Negotiation floor rule:** Never accept below €78,000 for your profile in Germany.

### What to Negotiate Beyond Salary
- Relocation allowance (€2,000–5,000 is standard)
- Visa + Blue Card processing cost covered by employer
- 30 days annual leave (standard in Germany)
- Remote/hybrid flexibility
- Training budget (€1,000–2,000/year common)
- Annual bonus structure
- Health insurance (employer covers 50% by law — confirm full coverage including diabetes)
