### 1.3 Diabetes and UAE Medical Fitness

**UAE medical fitness test requirements:**
- Chest X-ray (TB screening)
- HIV test (positive = visa rejection)
- Hepatitis B/C (positive = managed case-by-case)
- Basic blood panel

**Diabetes (Type 1 or 2, controlled):**
- Controlled diabetes (HbA1c under 8.0) = **UAE work visa approved** in practice for most employers
- UAE does not have a blanket ban on diabetics for work visas
- ADNOC (Abu Dhabi oil company) and government roles may be stricter — private sector / tech companies are fine
- Key: carry documentation from your Indian endocrinologist showing controlled management
- Bring 3-month supply of insulin/medications as UAE prescriptions take time to arrange

**Health Insurance in UAE:**
- Employers are legally required to provide health insurance in Dubai (mandatory since 2014)
- Major providers: Daman, AXA Gulf, Oman Insurance
- Pre-existing diabetes coverage: varies by policy. Negotiate pre-existing condition coverage in your employment contract. Many employer plans cover pre-existing conditions after 6 months waiting period. Ask for "no waiting period" for pre-existing conditions as part of negotiation.

- Indians are the **largest expatriate community in the UAE** (~3.5 million of 9.4 million population)

### 4.3 Hiring Cycles

**Q1 (January–March): BEST time to apply**
- New annual budgets approved; headcount approved
- Companies actively hiring to staff Q1 projects
- January and February: highest recruiter activity in UAE

**Q3 (July–September): Second-best window**
- Post-Ramadan (Ramadan 2026: ~Feb–Mar) hiring resumes
- Mid-year budget reviews release new headcount
- Avoid applying during Ramadan itself — hiring slows significantly

**Avoid:** June (summer slowdown), November–December (year-end freeze)

# SECTION 6 — DECISION MATRIX: GERMANY VS DUBAI

---

## Scenario A — Germany Offer Arrives First

**Evaluate the offer:**
- Is the salary ≥ €85,000 gross? If yes, lean toward accepting.
- Is the company Tier 1 (SAP, Allianz, Siemens, Zalando) or Tier 2?
- What is the visa type — EU Blue Card or regular work permit?
  - EU Blue Card: threshold ~€45,552 for shortage occupations (IT qualifies) in 2025 — your €90k+ easily qualifies
  - Blue Card → Permanent Residency in 21–33 months (faster than regular permit)

**Action if Germany offer arrives first:**
1. Accept if salary ≥ €85,000 and company is reputable
2. Notify any pending Dubai applications — you may choose to inform them or let them go
3. Begin visa / Blue Card processing immediately
4. Cancel Dubai job search (or pause it — keep optionality for 2 weeks until Germany visa confirmed)

---

## Scenario B — Dubai Offer Arrives First

**Evaluate the offer:**
- Is the salary ≥ AED 20,000/month? (Minimum acceptable)
- Is health insurance included with no waiting period for pre-existing conditions?
- Is the role a genuine step up (not a lateral move to a less prestigious company)?
- Does the contract have "no moonlighting" clauses that would prevent Germany job search?

**Action if Dubai offer arrives first:**
1. Negotiate: push for AED 22,000+ and pre-existing condition health coverage
2. Accept if the package meets minimums AND Germany is moving slowly (< 3 interviews at this point)
3. **Continue Germany job search remotely from Dubai** — this is the bridge strategy
4. Most German companies interview via video for initial rounds — being in Dubai doesn't stop the Germany search
5. Dubai gives you financial stability while you wait for the right Germany offer
6. Target: 12–18 months in Dubai, then transition to Germany once the right offer comes

**Dubai as a bridge strategy is viable if:** Salary ≥ AED 22,000, company is reputable on CV, and the role title is "Senior" or "Lead" (not junior).
