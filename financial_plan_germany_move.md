### 2.3 Health Insurance — Diabetes Consideration

**Important:** Germany has mandatory health insurance. As a Chancenkarte holder, you need health insurance from day one.

**Options:**

**Option A — German Statutory Health Insurance (GKV)**
- Techniker Krankenkasse (TK), AOK, DAK, Barmer
- Cost during job search (no income): approximately €200–230/month as a "voluntarily insured" member
- Pre-existing diabetes: COVERED fully under GKV — no exclusions allowed by German law
- Covers: doctor visits, hospitalization, insulin/medications (with small co-pay), specialist referrals
- **Recommendation: TK (Techniker Krankenkasse) — best reputation for English-language support, online portal, and diabetes management programs (e.g., Curaplan Herz Plus)**

**Option B — Travel/Expat Insurance (short-term, for visa only)**
- Companies: Feather, Ottonova, Allianz Care
- Cheaper short-term (€80–150/month) but excludes pre-existing conditions
- Only use this to meet visa entry requirement; switch to GKV immediately upon arrival

**Insulin / diabetes medication costs under GKV:**
- Prescription co-pay: €5–10 per prescription (capped at €10)
- Blood glucose meters and strips: covered with prescription
- Continuous Glucose Monitor (CGM): covered under GKV since 2022
- Annual cost: €60–120 in co-pays (not thousands — Germany is very good for this)

## Working 20 Hours/Week During Job Search

### 3.1 What the Chancenkarte Allows

The German Opportunity Card (Chancenkarte) allows:
- **Up to 20 hours/week** of part-time employment
- You can work for German employers without a separate work permit
- Exception: cannot work in the profession you are job-seeking for (i.e., cannot take a full Senior Java Lead role while on the card — must be below your qualification level, or freelance consulting is a grey area)
- In practice: IT contractors working 20hrs/week in a relevant field are rarely challenged if the work is clearly part-time/consultancy

---

### 6.2 How to Fund the Blocked Account

The Sperrkonto (blocked account) must be funded from India. Process:

1. Open the blocked account online with Expatrio, Fintiba, or Coracle (see Section 7)
2. Receive the IBAN of the blocked account
3. Transfer from your Indian bank account to this IBAN via Wise or bank wire
4. Wise allows INR → EUR international transfers to European IBANs directly
5. Confirm receipt with the provider; they issue the confirmation certificate needed for your visa application
6. Amount must be in the account before visa interview

**LRS (Liberalized Remittance Scheme) rules:** RBI allows Indian residents to remit up to USD 250,000/year abroad for permitted purposes. Education/maintenance abroad is a permitted purpose. No issue transferring €13,000 under LRS. File Form A2 with your bank.

---

### 6.3 Tax Implications of Sending Money to Germany

- **India:** No tax on transfers under LRS limit. No TDS on remittance for maintenance purposes. But: 5% TCS (Tax Collected at Source) applies on remittances above ₹7 lakh/year under LRS. TCS is refundable when you file ITR — it's not an additional tax.
- **Germany:** Receiving foreign funds is not taxable income. The blocked account funds are your own savings, not income.
- **Once you're a German tax resident** (after arrival and registration): your worldwide income becomes taxable in Germany. However, if you've left India and are no longer resident there, Indian taxes stop applying (except for India-sourced income). Get advice from a Steuerberater or cross-border tax specialist.

### Recommendation for Mayank

**Use Expatrio or Fintiba.** Both:
- Are operated entirely online (no need to visit a German bank)
- Accept account opening from India
- Issue the official confirmation letter required for the visa application
- Have English customer support
- Partner with real German banks (DKB, Deutsche Bank)

You cannot access more (hence "blocked") — this protects the funds as proof of financial means while ensuring you have a monthly stipend.
